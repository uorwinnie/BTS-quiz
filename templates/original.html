BTS , also known as Bangtan Boys, or 방탄소년단, is a seven-member musical act from South Korea. The group was established under the management of BigHit Music (now under HYBE Corporation), a South Korean entertainment company founded by songwriter and producer Bang Shi Hyuk. Despite the company's initial setbacks and limited resources, BigHit believed in their motto, “music and artist for healing,” and fostered an environment that allowed artists to develop their talents and to advance on their own authority. The members of BTS include Kim Namjoon (the leader), Kim Seokjin, Min Yoongi, Jung Hoseok, Park Jimin, Kim Taehyung, and Jeon Jungkook. More officially, each member is referred to by his stage name (in the same order): RM, Jin, Suga, J-hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook.
